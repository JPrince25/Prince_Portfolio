FORTRAN being the origin of most modern day languages that we use today just highlights how important it was. I especially enjoy not having to write all of my code in Assembly.
  • Innovation 2 - IBM Personal Computer (1981): The release of the IBM Personal Computer also has to be another great innovation. It was one of the first real PC's that was massively sold. The plan to shift and create devices that have a more commercial purpose has paved the way for incentives of technological innovations for the consumer to be more considered. After all, I've been writing this website on my PC.
  • Innovation 3 - Apple iPhone (2007): The iPhone release is my final important innovation. As we move into the mobile age, there is more of an incentive to create mobile software for these devices that artificially connect us around the world. Without the iPhone, this may not have come to be. The touchscreen along with the ability to run applications on the phone are a staple of today's culture and functions.
